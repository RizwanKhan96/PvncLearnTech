Support Request Priorities

We prioritize support requests based on their impact to teaching, learning, and operations. Understanding these priorities helps set appropriate expectations for response times.

  • Critical
    System-Wide Outages & Security Incidents
    Examples: Network down, email system offline, security breach, data loss affecting multiple users
    ‚è±Ô∏è Response Time: Immediate (within 15 minutes)
  • High
    Issues Preventing Teaching or Critical Work
    Examples: Classroom projector not working, device won't turn on, can't access critical software, account locked
    ‚è±Ô∏è Response Time: Within 2 hours during business hours
  • Medium
    Issues Causing Inconvenience but Workarounds Exist
    Examples: Printer issues, software not functioning as expected, slow performance, non-critical errors
    ‚è±Ô∏è Response Time: Within 1 business day
  • Low
    Requests, Questions, and Non-Urgent Issues
    Examples: Software installation requests, how-to questions, feature requests, general inquiries
    ‚è±Ô∏è Response Time: Within 2-3 business days
üí° Priority Assignment

Priority levels are assigned by the Help Desk based on impact and urgency. If your situation is urgent, please call (705-748-1811) rather than submitting a ticket to ensure immediate attention.

What Information to Include

Providing complete information helps us resolve your issue faster. When contacting the Help Desk, please include:

Required Information

  • Your name and contact information (email and phone number)
  • Your role (teacher, student, staff, administrator)
  • Your school or location
  • Device information if applicable (asset tag, serial number, or device name)
  • Clear description of the problem - what's not working?
  • When the problem started - was it sudden or gradual?
  • What you've already tried - any troubleshooting steps taken?

Helpful Additional Information

  • Error messages - exact wording or screenshots
  • Screenshots or photos of the issue
  • Steps to reproduce - what actions lead to the problem?
  • Frequency - does it happen every time or intermittently?
  • Impact - how is this affecting your work?
  • Urgency - do you need this resolved by a specific date/time?

Common Issues & Quick Solutions

I forgot my password. How do I reset it?

Visit Microsoft's password reset page and follow the prompts. If you're unable to reset it yourself, contact the Help Desk at 705-748-1811 or email helpdesk@pvnccdsb.on.ca with your name and account details.

My device won't turn on. What should I do?

First, ensure it's charged - plug it in for at least 30 minutes. Try holding the power button for 10-15 seconds. If still unresponsive, contact the Help Desk immediately, especially if you need the device for teaching or urgent work.

I can't connect to Wi-Fi. How do I fix this?

Try turning Wi-Fi off and on, or restart your device. Make sure you're connecting to the correct network (PVNCCDSB-Staff or PVNCCDSB-Student). If issues persist, check with colleagues to see if it's a widespread problem, then contact the Help Desk.

The projector/smartboard isn't working in my classroom.

Check that it's powered on and the correct input source is selected. Ensure cables are securely connected. Try restarting the projector and your computer. If unresolved, call the Help Desk (705-748-1811) immediately as this is a high-priority issue affecting instruction.

I need software installed. How do I request it?

Submit a ticket through the Help Desk portal or email helpdesk@pvnccdsb.on.ca with: software name and version, educational purpose, number of users who need it, and any licensing information. Allow 5-10 business days for evaluation and installation.

My email isn't working / I'm not receiving emails.

Check your internet connection first. Try accessing email from a different device or browser. Check your mailbox isn't full (Storage settings in Gmail). Verify the email address is correct. If issues continue, contact the Help Desk with specific error messages.

How do I report a damaged or lost device?

Report immediately to your school office or contact the Help Desk. Provide your name, device asset tag (if known), description of damage or circumstances of loss, and date/time of incident. For stolen devices, also file a police report.

I'm experiencing slow computer performance.

Close unused programs and browser tabs. Restart your computer (this often helps!). Check for pending Windows updates. Ensure you have at least 10GB free disk space. If problems persist after these steps, submit a Help Desk ticket.

Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Our commitment to you:

  • Acknowledgment: All tickets acknowledged within 4 business hours
  • Communication: Regular updates provided for ongoing issues
  • Resolution targets: Based on priority level (see above)
  • Follow-up: Confirmation that your issue is resolved
  • Escalation: Complex issues escalated to senior technicians or vendors as needed
üí° Setting Expectations

Response times are goals, not guarantees. Complex issues may require additional time for research, testing, or coordination with vendors. We'll keep you informed throughout the process.
