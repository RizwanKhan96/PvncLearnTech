This comprehensive guide will help you print documents from your PVNCCDSB Chromebook to school printers or your home printer.

Printing from Chromebooks

Chromebooks use Google Cloud Print and direct network printing to send documents to printers. At PVNCCDSB schools, printers are pre-configured for easy access. You can also print to your home printer when working remotely.

What You Can Print From

  • Google Docs, Sheets, & Slides - Direct printing from Google Workspace apps
  • Web Pages - Print from any website using Chrome
  • PDFs - Open and print PDF files
  • Images - Print photos and graphics
  • Email - Print messages and attachments from Gmail
‚ö†Ô∏è Print Responsibly

Be mindful of paper usage. Preview your document before printing, print only what you need, and consider using duplex (double-sided) printing when available to conserve resources.

Setting Up Your Printer

At School (PVNCCDSB Network)

School printers are automatically configured on managed Chromebooks. No setup required!

1

Connect to School Wi-Fi

Ensure your Chromebook is connected to the school network:

  • Students: Connect to PVNC-Student
  • Staff: Connect to PVNC-Staff

School printers will automatically appear in your print menu when connected to the school network.

At Home (Personal Printer)

1

Check Printer Compatibility

Most modern printers work with Chromebooks. Your printer should:

  • Support Wi-Fi or network printing, OR
  • Support USB connection (some Chromebooks), OR
  • Be a Cloud Print-ready printer
2

Add Your Printer

To add a home printer:

  1. Click the time in the bottom-right corner
  2. Click the Settings gear icon
  3. Scroll down and click Advanced
  4. Under "Printing," click Printers
  5. Click Add Printer
  6. Select your printer from the list or enter its IP address
  7. Click Add
üí° Finding Your Printer's IP Address

Most printers display their IP address on a small LCD screen, in the network settings menu, or on a printed configuration page. Consult your printer's manual for specific instructions.

How to Print Documents

Method 1: Printing from Google Docs, Sheets, or Slides

1

Open the Print Dialog

With your document open:

  • Click File ‚Üí Print, OR
  • Press Ctrl + P on your keyboard
2

Select Your Printer

In the print dialog:

  • Click the Destination dropdown
  • Select your printer from the list
  • School printers will be listed by location (e.g., "Library-Printer", "Office-Printer")
3

Configure Print Settings

Adjust your print options as needed:

  • Copies: Number of copies to print
  • Pages: All pages or specific page range
  • Layout: Portrait or Landscape orientation
  • Color: Color or Black & White
  • Paper size: Usually Letter (8.5" x 11")
4

Preview and Print

Before printing:

  • Review the print preview on the right side
  • Check that everything looks correct
  • Click the Print button

Your document will be sent to the printer.

Method 2: Printing from Chrome Browser

1

Open Print Menu

While viewing a web page or PDF:

  • Press Ctrl + P, OR
  • Click the three-dot menu ‚Üí Print
2

Select Printer and Options

Choose your printer and configure settings just like printing from Google Docs.

Tip: For web pages, click "More settings" to adjust margins and remove headers/footers.

Common Print Options Explained

Pages

  • All: Prints entire document
  • Custom: Enter specific pages (e.g., "1-3, 5, 8-10")

Layout

  • Portrait: Vertical orientation (standard for letters)
  • Landscape: Horizontal orientation (good for wide tables)

Color

  • Color: Full color printing (uses more ink)
  • Black & White: Grayscale printing (economical)

Pages per Sheet

  • 1: One page per sheet (normal)
  • 2: Two pages per sheet (saves paper)
  • 4: Four pages per sheet (for handouts)

Two-Sided (Duplex)

  • One-sided: Prints on one side only
  • Two-sided: Prints on both sides (saves paper)
‚úÖ Print Preview

Always use the print preview to check your document before printing. This helps you catch formatting issues and avoid wasting paper.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Printer Not Appearing

  • At school: Verify you're connected to PVNC-Student or PVNC-Staff Wi-Fi
  • At home: Ensure printer is powered on and connected to same Wi-Fi network
  • Try refreshing the printer list in print dialog
  • Restart your Chromebook and try again

Print Job Stuck or Not Printing

  • Check printer for paper jams or low paper
  • Verify printer has ink/toner
  • Cancel the print job and try printing again
  • At school: Ask office staff if printer needs attention

Document Formatting Issues

  • Check page orientation (Portrait vs. Landscape)
  • Adjust margins in "More settings"
  • Try "Save as PDF" first, then print the PDF
  • Ensure correct paper size is selected

Can't Add Home Printer

  • Verify printer supports network printing
  • Check that Chromebook and printer are on same Wi-Fi network
  • Try using printer's IP address instead of auto-detect
  • Consult printer manufacturer's Chromebook setup guide
‚ö†Ô∏è Save as PDF Option

If you're having trouble printing, you can always save the document as a PDF to Google Drive and print it later. In the print dialog, select "Save to Google Drive" as the destination.

Best Practices for Printing

  • Preview before printing: Always check print preview to avoid wasted paper
  • Print only what you need: Use page range option to print specific pages
  • Use duplex printing: Print double-sided when possible to save paper
  • Choose black & white: Unless color is necessary, grayscale is more economical
  • Adjust pages per sheet: For reference materials, consider 2 pages per sheet
  • Check printer location: At school, make sure you're printing to the nearest printer

Need Help?

If you continue to experience printing issues:

  • At school: Ask your teacher or visit the school office
  • Network printers: Contact IT for printer setup assistance
  • Home printer: Consult your printer's manual or manufacturer support
  • Chromebook issues: Submit a help desk ticket
