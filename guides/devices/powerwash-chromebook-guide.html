How to Powerwash a Chromebook

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Warning

Powerwashing will erase all data stored locally on your Chromebook. Make sure all important files are saved to Google Drive or backed up elsewhere before proceeding. This action cannot be undone.

What is a Powerwash?

A Powerwash is Chrome OS's version of a factory reset. It completely wipes your Chromebook's local storage and returns it to the state it was in when you first received it. This includes:

  • All downloaded files in the Downloads folder
  • All locally installed apps and extensions
  • Saved Wi-Fi networks and passwords
  • All settings and preferences
  • Browser history, cookies, and cache

What Powerwash Does NOT Delete

Files stored in your Google Drive are safe because they're stored in the cloud, not on your Chromebook:

  • Google Drive files (Docs, Sheets, Slides, etc.)
  • Gmail emails and contacts
  • Google Calendar events
  • Chrome bookmarks synced to your Google account
  • Google Photos
‚ÑπÔ∏è About This Process

The Powerwash process typically takes 5-10 minutes to complete. Your Chromebook will restart automatically when finished, and you'll need to go through the initial setup process again.

When Should You Powerwash?

Consider powerwashing your Chromebook if you're experiencing:

Performance Issues

  • Chromebook is running very slowly
  • Apps or browser tabs freeze frequently
  • System becomes unresponsive often
  • Boot time has increased significantly

Software Problems

  • Persistent error messages
  • Apps won't install or update properly
  • Chrome OS updates fail repeatedly
  • Settings won't save or keep reverting

Before Returning Device

  • Returning Chromebook to school at end of year
  • Transferring device to another student
  • Selling or donating your personal Chromebook
  • Device is being repaired or replaced

Security Concerns

  • Someone else had access to your device
  • Suspicious activity or potential malware
  • Want to ensure complete data removal
‚ö†Ô∏è Try These First

Before powerwashing, try these simpler solutions:

  • Restart your Chromebook (turn it off and on)
  • Clear browsing data (Ctrl + Shift + Delete)
  • Remove unnecessary extensions
  • Delete downloaded files you no longer need

If these don't help, then proceed with powerwashing.

Before You Powerwash: Important Preparation

1

Backup Your Files

Save any important files to Google Drive:

  • Open the Files app
  • Navigate to Downloads folder
  • Select files you want to keep
  • Right-click and choose Copy to > Google Drive
  • Wait for upload to complete (check Drive to confirm)

Double-check: Verify files appear in Google Drive before proceeding.

2

Note Your Wi-Fi Passwords

You'll need to reconnect to Wi-Fi networks after powerwashing:

  • Write down your home Wi-Fi network name and password
  • School networks will require re-authentication
  • Any other networks you regularly use
3

Make Note of Important Settings

Consider writing down:

  • Printer settings (if you added a home printer)
  • Custom accessibility settings
  • Important browser extensions you use
  • Any specific preferences you'll want to restore
4

Charge Your Chromebook

Ensure your Chromebook has at least 50% battery or is plugged into power. The powerwash process should not be interrupted.

Before continuing, confirm:

  • ‚úì All important files are backed up to Google Drive
  • ‚úì You have your Wi-Fi passwords written down
  • ‚úì Chromebook is charged or plugged in
  • ‚úì You understand this will erase all local data

How to Powerwash Your Chromebook

1

Open Settings

Click the time in the bottom-right corner of your screen, then click the Settings gear icon.

2

Navigate to Reset Settings

In the Settings window:

  • Scroll down to the bottom of the left sidebar
  • Click Advanced to expand options
  • Click Reset settings
3

Select Powerwash

In the Reset settings section:

  • Look for "Powerwash"
  • Click Powerwash
  • A dialog box will appear explaining what will be erased
4

Confirm Powerwash

Read the warning carefully, then:

  • Click Restart to begin the powerwash
  • Another confirmation may appear - click Powerwash or Continue

This is your last chance to cancel! Once you confirm, the process cannot be stopped or reversed.

5

Wait for Process to Complete

Your Chromebook will:

  • Restart automatically
  • Display a progress screen (5-10 minutes)
  • Restart again when finished
  • Show the initial setup screen

Do not: Turn off your Chromebook, close the lid, or unplug it during this process.

Alternative Method: Powerwash from Login Screen

If you can't access Settings (because Chrome OS won't boot properly), you can powerwash from the login screen:

  1. On the login screen, press Ctrl + Alt + Shift + R
  2. Click Restart in the dialog box
  3. In the box that appears, select Powerwash > Continue
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions

After Powerwashing: Initial Setup

1

Welcome Screen

After powerwashing completes, you'll see the initial Chrome OS welcome screen:

  • Select your language
  • Select your keyboard layout
  • Click Let's go
2

Connect to Wi-Fi

Connect to your Wi-Fi network:

  • Select your network from the list
  • Enter the password (you wrote this down earlier)
  • At school: Connect to PVNC-Student or PVNC-Staff
3

Sign In with Your Google Account

Sign in with your PVNCCDSB Google account:

  • Students: studentnumber@mypvnccdsb.ca
  • Staff: firstname.lastname@pvnccdsb.on.ca

Enter your password and follow any additional prompts.

4

Wait for Sync

After signing in, Chrome OS will automatically:

  • Restore your Chrome bookmarks
  • Reinstall synced extensions (if enabled)
  • Apply your Chrome settings
  • Connect to Google Drive

This process takes a few minutes. Be patient and let it complete.

5

Restore Your Settings

You'll need to manually reconfigure some settings:

  • Wallpaper and theme preferences
  • Printer settings (re-add home printers)
  • Accessibility options
  • Display and sound preferences
‚úÖ Powerwash Complete!

Your Chromebook is now restored to factory settings. All your Google Drive files should be accessible, and your device should run more smoothly.

What If Something Goes Wrong?

Powerwash Won't Start

  • Make sure you're signed in with your Google account
  • Try the keyboard shortcut method (Ctrl + Alt + Shift + R from login screen)
  • Perform a hard reset first: Hold Refresh + Power for 10 seconds, then try again

Process Gets Stuck

  • Wait at least 20 minutes - some Chromebooks take longer
  • If truly frozen, perform hard reset (Refresh + Power) and start over
  • Contact IT support if problem persists

Can't Sign In After Powerwash

  • Verify you're using the correct PVNCCDSB email address
  • Check that Wi-Fi connection is active
  • Try password reset if you forgot password
  • For school Chromebooks, IT may need to re-enroll device

Files Are Missing

  • Check Google Drive - files should be there if backed up
  • Files only in Downloads folder cannot be recovered
  • Check "Trash" in Google Drive for accidentally deleted files

Need Help?

If you encounter problems during or after powerwashing:

  • School Chromebook issues: Contact your teacher or school office
  • Technical problems: Submit an IT support ticket
  • Lost files: Unfortunately, local files deleted during powerwash cannot be recovered
  • Login issues: See our Password Reset Guide
