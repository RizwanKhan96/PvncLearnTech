This comprehensive guide will help you set up and manage Google Classroom effectively for organizing coursework, distributing assignments, grading student work, and fostering communication with your students.

What is Google Classroom?

Google Classroom is a free web service developed by Google for schools that helps teachers organize assignments, boost collaboration, and foster communication with students. Key features include:

  • Class Management - Organize all your classes, assignments, and materials in one central location
  • Assignment Distribution - Create and distribute assignments digitally with automatic organization
  • Efficient Grading - Grade and provide feedback on student work quickly and efficiently
  • Communication Tools - Post announcements, answer questions, and engage with students
  • Google Integration - Seamless integration with Google Docs, Drive, Calendar, and Gmail
  • Time-Saving - Automatic file organization and grade export to reduce administrative tasks

Getting Started

1

Access Google Classroom

You can access Google Classroom in multiple ways:

  • Go directly to classroom.google.com
  • Click the Google Classroom app in your Chromebook launcher
  • Access it through the Google Apps menu (waffle icon) from Gmail or Drive

Sign in with your PVNCCDSB Google account if prompted.

https://classroom.google.com
2

Choose Teacher Role

When you first access Classroom, select "I'm a teacher" to continue to your teacher dashboard.

Creating a Class

Setting up a new class in Google Classroom is quick and straightforward:

1

Click the "+" Button

In the top-right corner of Google Classroom, click the "+" (plus) button and select "Create class".

2

Enter Class Details

Fill in your class information:

  • Class name: Required (e.g., "Grade 10 Mathematics")
  • Section: Optional (e.g., "Period 1", "Block A")
  • Subject: Optional (e.g., "Mathematics")
  • Room: Optional (e.g., "Room 203")

Click "Create" when finished.

3

Invite Students

Add students to your class in two ways:

  • Class Code: Share the automatically generated class code with students
  • Direct Invite: Go to People tab ‚Üí click "Invite students" ‚Üí enter student email addresses

Tip: The class code is visible on the Stream page and can be reset or disabled at any time.

‚úÖ Class Created!

Your class is now ready. You can customize the theme, add co-teachers, and start posting assignments and materials.

Managing Your Class

Each Google Classroom has four main sections accessible from the top navigation bar:

Stream

The Stream is your class's main page where you can:

  • Post announcements to all students
  • Share assignments and materials
  • View and respond to student comments
  • See upcoming assignments and due dates
  • Attach files, links, or YouTube videos to posts

Classwork

The Classwork tab allows you to:

  • Create and organize assignments by topic
  • Add class materials and resources
  • Create questions for discussion or quick polls
  • Reuse assignments from previous classes
  • Schedule assignments for future posting
  • View assignment submission status at a glance

People

The People tab lets you:

  • View all enrolled students
  • Add or remove students
  • Invite co-teachers or teaching assistants
  • Email individual students or the entire class
  • Manage student permissions

Grades

The Grades tab provides:

  • Overview of all student grades
  • Individual student grade tracking
  • Grade export to Google Sheets or CSV
  • Assignment grading access
  • Overall class performance metrics

Creating Assignments

1

Navigate to Classwork

Click the Classwork tab, then click "+ Create" and select:

  • Assignment: For graded work students submit
  • Quiz assignment: Create a Google Forms quiz
  • Question: For short answer or multiple choice discussions
  • Material: For reference materials (not submitted)
2

Configure Assignment Details

Fill in the assignment information:

  • Title: Clear, descriptive name
  • Instructions: Detailed directions for students
  • Points: Grade value (or mark as "Ungraded")
  • Due date: Optional deadline with time
  • Topic: Organize assignments by unit or subject
3

Add Materials & Resources

Attach supporting materials to your assignment:

  • Upload: Add files from your computer
  • Google Drive: Attach existing Drive files
  • YouTube: Embed instructional videos
  • Link: Add website URLs
  • Template: Create a Google Doc/Sheet/Slides template for students to copy

Pro Tip: Choose "Make a copy for each student" for templates to ensure individual work copies.

4

Assign to Students

Choose how to distribute your assignment:

  • Assign: Post immediately to all students
  • Schedule: Set a future date and time to auto-post
  • Save draft: Save for later editing
  • Assign to specific students: Select individual students or groups
‚ö†Ô∏è Before Assigning

Review all assignment details, due dates, and attached materials. Students receive notifications immediately when you post, so ensure everything is correct first.

Grading & Providing Feedback

1

Access Student Submissions

View student work by:

  • Clicking on the assignment in the Classwork tab
  • Reviewing the submission status (Turned in, Assigned, Graded)
  • Filtering by status to prioritize grading
2

Grade Student Work

For each student submission:

  • Open the student's submitted file or response
  • Add comments directly on Google Docs/Sheets/Slides
  • Use private comments for feedback visible only to that student
  • Enter the grade in the points field
  • Click "Return" to send graded work back to student

Efficiency Tip: Use keyboard shortcuts to navigate between students quickly.

3

Provide Rubric Feedback

Create and use rubrics for consistent grading:

  • Add a rubric when creating the assignment
  • Define criteria and point values
  • Select rubric levels when grading
  • Reuse rubrics across multiple assignments
  • Students can view rubric criteria before submitting

Communicating with Students

Class Announcements

Post announcements on the Stream that all students can see. Use this for:

  • Class reminders and important dates
  • General instructions or clarifications
  • Sharing resources or helpful links
  • Celebrating student achievements

Private Comments

On individual assignments, add private comments visible only to that student. Ideal for:

  • Personalized feedback on submitted work
  • Answering student-specific questions
  • Providing encouragement or guidance

Email Communication

Email students directly from the People tab:

  • Select individual students or entire class
  • Opens Gmail with recipients pre-populated
  • Maintains record in your sent mail
üí° Best Practices
  • Organize assignments using topics (units, chapters, themes)
  • Schedule assignments in advance during planning time
  • Reuse successful assignments from previous terms
  • Enable "Students can post and comment" for collaborative discussions
  • Export grades regularly to maintain external records
  • Use Google Meet integration for virtual office hours

Need Help?

If you encounter any issues with Google Classroom:

  • Can't create a class: Verify you have teacher permissions through PVNCCDSB IT
  • Students can't join: Check that the class code is active and hasn't been disabled
  • Can't see student work: Ensure students have submitted (check assignment status)
  • Can't access Classroom: Verify you're signed in with your PVNCCDSB staff account
  • Grade export issues: Try exporting to Google Sheets first, then download as CSV
  • Co-teacher access problems: Re-invite co-teachers using their @pvnccdsb.on.ca email
