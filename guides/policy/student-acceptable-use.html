This document outlines the expectations and guidelines for students using technology at PVNCCDSB. These rules help keep everyone safe, protect privacy, and ensure technology is used for learning. All students must follow these guidelines when using school devices, networks, and online resources.

Welcome to PVNCCDSB Technology

Technology is an important part of learning at PVNCCDSB. You'll use computers, tablets, the internet, email, and online tools to support your education. With access to these resources comes responsibility to use them properly, safely, and respectfully.

This policy applies when you:

  • Use school-owned devices (Chromebooks, laptops, tablets, computers)
  • Connect to school Wi-Fi networks
  • Access your PVNCCDSB email account
  • Use Google Workspace (Classroom, Docs, Drive, etc.)
  • Visit websites for schoolwork
  • Participate in online learning platforms
  • Use personal devices on school property or for school activities
‚ö†Ô∏è Important

By using PVNCCDSB technology, you agree to follow these guidelines. Violations may result in loss of technology privileges, disciplinary action, and in serious cases, involvement of law enforcement.

Your Rights as a Technology User

As a PVNCCDSB student, you have the right to:

  • Access technology: Use school devices and internet for educational purposes
  • Digital learning tools: Access online resources, software, and platforms needed for your classes
  • Email communication: A PVNCCDSB email account for school-related communication
  • Safe environment: Use technology in an environment free from cyberbullying and harassment
  • Privacy protection: Have your personal information protected according to privacy laws
  • Technical support: Receive help when experiencing technology issues
  • Fair treatment: Be treated equitably and have concerns addressed respectfully

Your Responsibilities as a Technology User

With these rights come important responsibilities:

General Responsibilities

  • Follow all rules: Adhere to this policy and all school rules when using technology
  • Use for learning: Technology is provided for educational purposes - keep use focused on schoolwork
  • Respect others: Treat all users with dignity and respect in online interactions
  • Protect devices: Handle all devices carefully and report damage immediately
  • Report problems: Tell a teacher or staff member if you encounter inappropriate content or cyberbullying
  • Keep it clean: Don't eat or drink near devices; keep your workspace tidy
  • Be honest: Take responsibility for your actions online and offline

Account Security Responsibilities

  • Protect your password: Never share your password with anyone, even friends
  • Log out: Always log out when finished, especially on shared devices
  • Lock your screen: Lock your screen when stepping away from your device
  • Report suspicious activity: Tell a teacher if someone accessed your account without permission
  • Use only your account: Never use someone else's login credentials

Using School Devices Properly

Device Care and Handling

  • Carry carefully: Always use two hands; never carry by the screen
  • Use protective cases: Keep cases on devices at all times
  • Avoid extreme conditions: Don't leave devices in hot cars, cold temperatures, or wet areas
  • Clean properly: Use only approved cleaning methods (ask your teacher)
  • Charge responsibly: Charge devices overnight; bring charged devices to school
  • Store safely: Put devices in designated storage areas when not in use
  • Report damage immediately: Tell your teacher right away if a device is damaged

What NOT to Do with Devices

  • ‚ùå Don't place anything on top of a closed device
  • ‚ùå Don't remove or damage identification labels, stickers, or asset tags
  • ‚ùå Don't attempt to repair devices yourself
  • ‚ùå Don't loan your device to others
  • ‚ùå Don't alter device settings without permission
  • ‚ùå Don't add unauthorized stickers or decorations
  • ‚ùå Don't expose devices to magnets
  • ‚ùå Don't force connections or cables

Appropriate Internet Use

‚úÖ

DO: Acceptable Activities

  • Access websites assigned by teachers
  • Research topics for school projects
  • Use educational videos and tutorials
  • Access Google Classroom and learning platforms
  • Use appropriate reference websites
  • Download files assigned by teachers
  • Collaborate on shared documents
  • Use school-approved educational apps

DON'T: Prohibited Activities

  • Access inappropriate or offensive content
  • Visit gaming websites (unless educational)
  • Stream entertainment videos during class
  • Shop online or browse e-commerce sites
  • Use social media during instructional time
  • Download unauthorized software or apps
  • Access proxy or VPN services
  • Attempt to bypass internet filters

Internet Safety Guidelines

  • Protect personal information: Never share your full name, address, phone number, or school name on public websites
  • Be skeptical: Not everything online is true - verify information with multiple sources
  • Think before clicking: Don't click on suspicious links or pop-ups
  • Report inappropriate content: Tell a teacher immediately if you see something concerning
  • Respect copyrights: Only use images, music, and content you have permission to use
  • Cite your sources: Give credit when using information from websites
‚ö†Ô∏è Content Filtering

All internet activity on school networks is monitored and filtered. Websites are automatically blocked if they contain inappropriate material. Attempting to bypass filters is a serious violation and will result in consequences.

Email and Communication Guidelines

Appropriate Email Use

Your PVNCCDSB email account is for school-related communication only.

Use Your Email To:

  • Communicate with teachers and school staff
  • Collaborate with classmates on group projects
  • Submit assignments when instructed
  • Receive notifications from Google Classroom
  • Access educational accounts that require school email
  • Communicate about school activities and events

Do NOT Use Email For:

  • Personal conversations with friends (use personal email for that)
  • Forwarding chain letters or spam
  • Signing up for non-educational websites or services
  • Harassing, threatening, or bullying others
  • Sharing inappropriate content
  • Pretending to be someone else

Email Etiquette and Best Practices

  • Use appropriate language: Write professionally and respectfully
  • Use proper grammar: Avoid text-speak; write in complete sentences
  • Include a subject line: Make it clear what your email is about
  • Be respectful: Use polite greetings and closings
  • Think before sending: Re-read emails before hitting send
  • Respond promptly: Reply to teacher emails within 24 hours
  • Don't SHOUT: Using ALL CAPS is considered rude
üí° Privacy Notice

School email is not private. Administrators can view email contents if there's a concern about safety or policy violations. Never write anything in school email you wouldn't want teachers or administrators to see.

Social Media and Online Conduct

Social Media During School Hours

  • Social media access is blocked during instructional time
  • You may access social media before/after school or during lunch (if age-appropriate)
  • Never access social media during class unless specifically assigned by teacher
  • Personal devices used for social media must follow all school rules

Representing PVNCCDSB Online

Remember that your online actions reflect on you and your school:

  • Be positive: Represent your school community respectfully
  • Think before posting: Online posts are permanent, even if deleted
  • Protect privacy: Don't post photos or information about classmates without permission
  • Avoid conflicts: Don't engage in online arguments or drama
  • Report cyberbullying: Tell a trusted adult if you or someone else is being bullied online

Cyberbullying is NOT Tolerated

Cyberbullying includes:

  • Sending mean, threatening, or hurtful messages
  • Posting embarrassing photos or videos of others
  • Spreading rumors online
  • Creating fake accounts to impersonate someone
  • Excluding someone from online groups to hurt them
  • Sharing private information to embarrass someone
üö® Zero Tolerance for Cyberbullying

PVNCCDSB has zero tolerance for cyberbullying. Any student who cyberbullies others faces serious consequences, including suspension. If you're being cyberbullied or witness it, report it immediately to a teacher, counselor, or administrator.

Consequences for Policy Violations

‚öñÔ∏è Progressive Discipline

PVNCCDSB uses progressive discipline for technology violations. Consequences increase with repeated violations or severity of the offense.

Level 1: Minor Violations

Examples: Off-task internet use, playing games during class, not logging out

Consequences may include:

  • Verbal warning from teacher
  • Loss of device privileges for remainder of class
  • Parent/guardian notification
  • Written reflection on appropriate technology use

Level 2: Moderate Violations

Examples: Repeated off-task use, attempting to bypass filters, using someone else's account, sharing inappropriate content

Consequences may include:

  • Loss of technology privileges for extended period (days or weeks)
  • Parent/guardian meeting
  • After-school detention
  • Technology use contract
  • Administrative involvement

Level 3: Serious Violations

Examples: Cyberbullying, accessing inappropriate content, hacking attempts, theft of information, threats

Consequences may include:

  • Loss of all technology privileges for semester or year
  • In-school or out-of-school suspension
  • Police involvement (for illegal activities)
  • Expulsion (in extreme cases)
  • Financial responsibility for damages
  • Loss of participation in extracurricular activities
‚ö†Ô∏è Damage and Loss

Students and parents/guardians are financially responsible for damage to or loss of school devices due to negligence or misuse. Intentional damage may also result in additional disciplinary consequences.

Device Damage and Loss Procedures

If Your Device is Damaged

  1. Stop using it immediately to prevent further damage
  2. Tell your teacher as soon as possible
  3. Complete an incident report describing what happened
  4. Do not attempt repairs - only IT staff can repair devices
  5. A loaner device may be provided while yours is repaired

If Your Device is Lost or Stolen

  1. Report immediately to your teacher or school office
  2. File a police report if device was stolen (required)
  3. Complete school forms documenting the loss
  4. Parents may be responsible for replacement costs

Getting Technology Help

For Technical Issues

  • Ask your teacher first - they can often resolve simple issues
  • Visit the school library/tech office for assistance
  • Submit a help desk ticket for complex problems
  • Be patient - IT staff support all schools and prioritize issues

For Policy Questions

  • Ask your teacher or school administrator
  • Review this policy document
  • Contact the school office for clarification

Reporting Problems and Concerns

If You See or Experience:

  • Inappropriate content: Tell your teacher immediately; don't try to close it yourself
  • Cyberbullying: Report to a teacher, counselor, or administrator right away
  • Someone accessing your account: Change your password and report to a teacher
  • Another student violating policies: Don't confront them; report to a staff member
  • Suspicious emails or messages: Don't respond; show a teacher
  • Technical problems: Report to your teacher or IT support
‚úÖ Reporting is Important

You will never get in trouble for reporting a problem or concern, even if you accidentally accessed inappropriate content. We want to keep everyone safe, and that requires students to speak up when they see issues.
