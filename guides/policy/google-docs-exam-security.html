üö® Critical Security Notice

Improper handling of exam materials in Google Docs can lead to academic integrity violations, unauthorized access to assessments, and compromised student evaluations. This guide outlines essential security practices to protect your exams and tests.

Why Exam Security Matters

Google Docs is a powerful tool for creating and managing educational content, but exams and tests require special security considerations:

  • Academic Integrity: Prevents students from accessing exams before test day
  • Fairness: Ensures all students have equal conditions for assessment
  • Privacy: Protects confidential educational materials
  • Professional Responsibility: Maintains trust in assessment processes
  • Legal Compliance: Meets privacy legislation requirements (MFIPPA)

Common Security Risks with Google Docs

‚ö†Ô∏è

Risk #1: Accidental Public Sharing

Most common security breach

The Problem: A single click can make your exam accessible to "anyone with the link" or even publicly searchable on the internet.

Real Scenario: Teacher shares exam link with colleague via email. Email is forwarded to department list. Student on list accesses exam before test day.

Impact: Entire class assessment compromised, requiring new exam creation.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Risk #2: Version History Exposure

Hidden content may still be accessible

The Problem: Google Docs keeps complete version history. Even if you delete answer keys or solutions, they remain accessible through "Version history".

Real Scenario: Teacher creates exam with answer key in same document, then deletes answers. Student with edit access views version history to see deleted answers.

Impact: Students can access solutions even after they've been "removed".

‚ö†Ô∏è

Risk #3: Shared Drive Misconfiguration

Department-wide access to sensitive materials

The Problem: Placing exams in Shared Drives with broad permissions gives many users access to confidential materials.

Real Scenario: Exam stored in "Grade 10 Math" shared drive accessible to all math teachers, student teachers, and educational assistants.

Impact: Expanded access increases risk of accidental or intentional leak.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Risk #4: Notification Leaks

Email notifications reveal document existence

The Problem: When you share documents or add comments, Google sends email notifications that can alert unintended recipients.

Real Scenario: Teacher adds student name in comment to note question difficulty. Student receives email notification with link to exam document.

Impact: Students become aware of exam location and may attempt access.

Security Best Practices

‚úÖ

Best Practice #1: Keep Exams Private to Your Drive

Default security posture

What to Do:

  • Store ALL exam materials in My Drive, not Shared Drives
  • Create a dedicated folder: "Assessments - CONFIDENTIAL"
  • Never share this folder with students
  • Limit sharing to specific trusted colleagues only when necessary

Why It Works: Documents in My Drive are private by default. Only you can access them unless you explicitly grant permission.

‚úÖ

Best Practice #2: Separate Answer Keys Completely

Physical separation of sensitive materials

What to Do:

  • Create answer keys in separate documents
  • Never include answers in the same document as the exam (even temporarily)
  • Name answer key documents clearly: "[Exam Name] - ANSWER KEY - CONFIDENTIAL"
  • Store answer keys in separate folder from student-facing exams

Why It Works: Complete separation prevents version history exploits and accidental sharing of solutions.

‚úÖ

Best Practice #3: Use Explicit Sharing Only

Control exactly who has access

What to Do:

  • NEVER use "Anyone with the link" for exam materials
  • NEVER make exams publicly accessible
  • Share only with specific email addresses when needed
  • Use "Viewer" permission (not "Editor") for colleagues reviewing exams
  • Remove access immediately after review is complete

Why It Works: Explicit sharing creates audit trail and prevents link-based access.

‚úÖ

Best Practice #4: Disable Link Sharing and Downloading

Additional protection layer

What to Do:

  • Open sharing settings for exam document
  • Click gear icon (‚öôÔ∏è) in sharing dialog
  • Uncheck "Viewers and commenters can see the option to download, print, and copy"
  • This prevents colleagues from downloading local copies that may be less secure

Why It Works: Limits distribution of exam materials beyond your Google Drive control.

Proper Sharing Settings: Step by Step

Checking Current Sharing Status

  1. Open your exam document in Google Docs
  2. Click the Share button (top-right corner)
  3. Review the "General access" section
  4. Verify it shows "Restricted - Only people added can open with this link"
  5. If it says anything else (Anyone with link, Anyone at PVNCCDSB, Public), change it immediately

Secure Sharing with Colleagues

When you need to share an exam with another teacher for review:

  1. Click Share button
  2. In "Add people and groups" field, enter colleague's PVNCCDSB email address
  3. Change permission to "Viewer" (not Editor unless they need to edit)
  4. Uncheck "Notify people" if you don't want email sent
  5. Click Send or Done
  6. After review, remove their access:
    • Click Share again
    • Find colleague's name in list
    • Click their permission level ‚Üí Select Remove access
‚ö†Ô∏è Email Notification Risk

When you share a document, Google sends an email notification by default. This email contains a direct link to the document. If the recipient forwards this email or it's intercepted, unauthorized people could potentially access the link. For highly sensitive exams, consider:

  • Unchecking "Notify people" when sharing
  • Sharing link verbally or via secure channel instead
  • Using temporary access that you remove after viewing

Distributing Exams to Students

Option 1: Google Classroom Assignment (Recommended)

The most secure method for distributing exams digitally:

  1. In Google Classroom, create new Assignment
  2. Click Add ‚Üí Google Drive
  3. Select your exam document
  4. CRITICAL: Choose "Students can view file" or "Make a copy for each student"
  5. NEVER choose "Students can edit file" - this gives access to original
  6. Set assignment to Scheduled and select exam start time
  7. Only publish assignment when exam begins

Why This Works:

  • Students don't get access until you publish
  • Each student gets individual copy (can't see others' work)
  • Original exam document remains secure in your Drive
  • Classroom tracks submission timestamps

Option 2: Paper-Based Distribution

For high-stakes exams, consider printing from Google Docs:

  1. Print exam directly from Google Docs
  2. Distribute paper copies in supervised environment
  3. Collect all copies after exam (count them)
  4. Secure physical storage of completed exams

Benefits: No digital access risks, full control of materials

Option 3: Google Forms for Objective Assessments

For multiple-choice, short-answer, or fill-in-the-blank assessments:

  1. Create exam in Google Forms instead of Docs
  2. Enable "Restrict to PVNCCDSB users"
  3. Turn on "Limit to 1 response"
  4. Set Forms to accept responses only during exam time
  5. Share Form link only when exam begins

Security Features:

  • Auto-grades objective questions
  • Timestamps all submissions
  • Can shuffle question order for each student
  • No access to form content until you share link
üí° Pro Tip: Lockdown Browser

For high-stakes exams, consider using Google Forms with lockdown browser extensions or proctoring tools available through PVNCCDSB. Contact IT Help Desk for options.

After Exam Administration

Immediate Actions

  1. Unpublish Classroom assignment (if used) to prevent late access
  2. Close Google Form (if used) to stop accepting responses
  3. Revoke any temporary sharing granted to colleagues
  4. Move used exams to archived folder (not deleted)

Long-Term Storage

After exam marking is complete:

  • Move exam document to "Previous Exams" folder in your private Drive
  • Keep for reference but ensure it remains restricted
  • Update document title to include date/semester: "Math Midterm - Fall 2024"
  • Document any security incidents (unauthorized access attempts) and report to administration

Exam Reuse Considerations

If planning to reuse exam questions:

  • Create new document with modified questions (don't just edit original)
  • Assume students may have shared previous versions
  • Change question order, values, and scenarios significantly
  • Maintain previous versions for record-keeping

Pre-Exam Security Checklist

Complete Before Every Exam

  • Exam document is stored in My Drive, not Shared Drive
  • General access is set to "Restricted" (not "Anyone with link")
  • No students have been granted access to exam document
  • Answer key is in separate document from exam
  • Download/print/copy options are disabled for shared viewers
  • Exam folder is clearly labeled as CONFIDENTIAL
  • Any colleague access has been reviewed and is time-limited
  • No links to exam have been shared via email or chat
  • Google Classroom assignment is scheduled, not published yet
  • Backup copy exists in case of technical issues
  • Previous year's versions are archived and inaccessible to students
  • Physical printouts (if any) are secured in locked cabinet

What to Do If Security Is Breached

üö® If Exam Is Compromised

Take immediate action if you suspect students have accessed exam materials:

  1. Document everything: Take screenshots of sharing settings, access logs, etc.
  2. Revoke all access: Change sharing settings to maximum restriction
  3. Notify administration: Report to department head and vice-principal immediately
  4. Contact IT Help Desk: They can check access logs to determine who viewed document
  5. Prepare alternate assessment: You may need replacement exam
  6. Document academic integrity concern: If student involvement, follow board policy

Checking Access History

To see who has accessed your exam document:

  1. Open the document
  2. Click File ‚Üí Version history ‚Üí See version history
  3. Review names and timestamps of all viewers and editors
  4. Take screenshots for documentation
  5. Look for unexpected names or access outside normal hours

Remember these essential security practices:

  • Revoke access immediately after use
