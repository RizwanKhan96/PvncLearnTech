This walkthrough will help you set up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for your PVNCCDSB account. MFA adds an extra layer of security to protect your account and board data. Follow each step carefully to complete the setup.

What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

Multi-Factor Authentication (also called Two-Factor Authentication or 2FA) is a security feature that requires two forms of identification to access your account:

  • Something you know: Your password
  • Something you have: Your mobile phone or another authentication device

When you sign in, you'll enter your password as usual, then verify your identity using your phone. This prevents unauthorized access even if someone learns your password.

Why PVNCCDSB Requires MFA

  • Protects Student Data: Prevents unauthorized access to sensitive student information
  • Prevents Account Compromise: Even if your password is stolen, attackers cannot access your account
  • Regulatory Compliance: Meets privacy legislation requirements (MFIPPA, PHIPA)
  • Reduces Phishing Risk: Phishing attacks that steal passwords are ineffective without the second factor
  • Industry Standard: MFA is now standard security practice in education and business
‚ö†Ô∏è Important

MFA is mandatory for all PVNCCDSB staff and students accessing board systems. You must complete this setup to continue using your account for email, Google Workspace, and other board resources.

Step 1: Install Microsoft Authenticator App

1

Download and Install the App

The Microsoft Authenticator app is free and available on all major mobile platforms.

For iPhone/iPad Users:

  1. Open the App Store on your device
  2. Search for "Microsoft Authenticator"
  3. Tap Get, then Install
  4. Wait for the app to download and install
  5. Tap Open when installation completes

For Android Users:

  1. Open the Google Play Store on your device
  2. Search for "Microsoft Authenticator"
  3. Tap Install
  4. Wait for the app to download and install
  5. Tap Open when installation completes
üì± Screenshot: Microsoft Authenticator in App Store/Google Play Store
‚úÖ Verification

The official app is published by Microsoft Corporation. Make sure you're installing the correct app.

Step 2: Initial App Setup

2

Configure Initial App Settings

When you first open Microsoft Authenticator, you'll need to grant some permissions.

  1. Open the Microsoft Authenticator app
  2. Tap Accept when prompted about Terms & Conditions
  3. When asked to allow notifications:
    • Tap Allow (iOS) or OK (Android)
    • Notifications are required for push-based authentication
  4. If prompted to sign in with a Microsoft account:
    • Tap Skip for now (you'll add your account in the next step)
üì± Screenshot: Microsoft Authenticator welcome screen with "Add account" button
üí° Pro Tip

Enable notifications so you can approve sign-in requests with a single tap instead of typing codes. This makes authentication faster and more convenient.

Step 3: Add Your PVNCCDSB Account

3

Connect Your Account to Authenticator

Now you'll register your PVNCCDSB account with the Authenticator app.

On Your Computer:

  1. Open a web browser and go to aka.ms/mfasetup
  2. Sign in with your PVNCCDSB credentials:
    • Username: firstname.lastname@pvnccdsb.on.ca
    • Password: Your current password
  3. You'll see a message: "More information required"
  4. Click Next to begin setup
  5. On the "Keep your account secure" screen, click Next
  6. Select Mobile app as your verification method
  7. Select "Receive notifications for verification" (recommended)
  8. Click Set up
  9. A QR code will appear on your screen - keep this window open
üíª Screenshot: QR code displayed on computer screen

On Your Mobile Device:

  1. Open the Microsoft Authenticator app
  2. Tap the + (plus) button or Add account
  3. Select Work or school account
  4. Tap Scan a QR code
  5. Point your camera at the QR code on your computer screen
  6. Wait for the app to scan and add your account
  7. Your account will appear in the app with "PVNCCDSB" label
üì± Screenshot: Authenticator app showing PVNCCDSB account added
‚ö†Ô∏è Can't Scan the QR Code?

If you're having trouble scanning the QR code:

  • Make sure camera permissions are enabled for Authenticator
  • Try adjusting the distance between phone and screen
  • Increase screen brightness on your computer
  • Or tap "Enter code manually" and type in the code shown below the QR code

Step 4: Verify Your Setup

4

Test Authentication

You'll now verify that everything is working correctly.

  1. On your computer, click Next after scanning the QR code
  2. A notification will appear on your mobile device
  3. On your phone, tap the notification to open Authenticator
  4. Review the sign-in request details:
    • Account name (your email)
    • Sign-in location
    • Time of request
  5. If everything looks correct, tap Approve
  6. On your computer, you should see "Notification approved"
  7. Click Next, then Done
üì± Screenshot: Authentication approval notification on phone
‚úÖ Success!

Your Microsoft Authenticator is now set up and ready to use. You'll use this app every time you sign in to your PVNCCDSB account.

Step 5: Configure Additional Authentication Methods (Recommended)

5

Add Backup Authentication Methods

It's highly recommended to add alternative authentication methods in case you lose access to your phone.

Add a Phone Number:

  1. Go to aka.ms/mfasetup
  2. Sign in if prompted
  3. Click + Add sign-in method
  4. Select Phone from the dropdown
  5. Click Add
  6. Enter your mobile phone number (including area code)
  7. Choose whether to receive a text message or phone call
  8. Click Next
  9. Enter the verification code you receive
  10. Click Next, then Done

Add an Alternate Email (Optional):

  1. Click + Add sign-in method again
  2. Select Email
  3. Click Add
  4. Enter a personal email address (not your PVNCCDSB email)
  5. Click Next
  6. Check your email and enter the verification code
  7. Click Verify
üí° Why Add Backup Methods?

If you lose your phone or it breaks, backup authentication methods prevent you from being locked out of your account. Always maintain at least one backup method.

Using MFA to Sign In

Now that MFA is set up, here's what happens when you sign in:

  1. Enter your username and password as usual
  2. You'll see a message: "Approve sign-in request"
  3. Check your phone for a notification from Microsoft Authenticator
  4. Tap the notification to open the app
  5. Review the sign-in details (location, time, app name)
  6. Tap Approve if it's you signing in
  7. You'll be signed in on your computer
‚ö†Ô∏è Security Reminder

Only approve sign-in requests that you initiated. If you receive an unexpected notification, tap Deny and immediately change your password - someone may have your password.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Not Receiving Notifications

Solutions:

  • Check that notifications are enabled for Microsoft Authenticator in phone settings
  • Ensure phone has internet connection (Wi-Fi or cellular data)
  • Force close and reopen the Authenticator app
  • Try using the 6-digit code shown in the app instead (tap the account in Authenticator)
  • Use backup authentication method (phone call or text message)

Problem: Codes Don't Work

Solutions:

  • Ensure phone's date and time are set to automatic (codes are time-sensitive)
  • Enter the code quickly - they expire after 30 seconds
  • Make sure you're entering the code for the correct account
  • Try refreshing the code by tapping the account in Authenticator

Problem: Lost or Replaced Phone

Solutions:

  • Use backup authentication method (phone number or email) to sign in
  • Install Microsoft Authenticator on new phone
  • Go to aka.ms/mfasetup and remove old device
  • Add new device following steps 3-4 in this guide
  • If locked out completely, contact IT Help Desk to reset MFA

Problem: QR Code Won't Scan

Solutions:

  • Check camera permissions for Microsoft Authenticator
  • Clean phone camera lens
  • Increase computer screen brightness
  • Try manual code entry instead (tap "Can't scan?" or "Enter code manually")
